# Chetan Bhagat

![Chetan Bhagat](https://d3f4ult1.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/img_0824.jpg?w=1024)

Usually I don't read more than one book from same author. At least that's what happened in my initial reading era.

But lately, as I'm counting more birthdays, I started looking for more comfortable reads. Like a comfort food, I discovered my comfort genre.

They are detective stories. 

Now Chetan Bhagat is not known for detective stories. He is best known for his IIT/IIM love stories and all his protagonists are IITians. 

So when I first came across, The Girl in Room 105 in 2018 I was not very excited to pick it up. Because I thought he didn't have anything new to write, I assumed he again cooked a love story of elite IITians from middle class families. 

And then I saw this trailer,

![Trailer](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ydM-jqY7NDk)

It did tingle my curiosity (A filmy trailer for a book release ! That's new)

But I didn't want to read a cliché, so didn't bother picking it up. 

Two years later, as life happened I started on a silly journey. It is called U P S C.

I know, very silly of me !

Did my grind, did my time for the crime.

In 2024, after getting back into workforce in Hyderabad for 2 years then, I was at a bookstore and read first line from a book.

> 'I hate this stupid UPSC exam', I said, tossing my pen onto the table. <br>

This line stuck like a hook in my head. I was happy that there's a story with a protagonist preparing for and hating the process of preparing for the UPSC exam. That too in first person narrative!

I immediately checked the label again, 400 days by Chetan Bhagat. 

I was impressed that he finally entered an arena where at least 50% of the Indian youth is, unlike the elite 0.5% youth. (these numbers are arbitrary but you get the idea)

I bought it and finished it that week. 

Wonderful read, the protagonist Keshav was so relatable.

Then I immediately read the first instalment in the series, The Girl in Room 105. 

Fantastic read. True to its word, it's an un-love story. I never read anything like it before. 

And finally I ready the one released in between them, An Arranged Murder. I sensed by page 2 that it's not at par with other two. But finished it anyway. 

Chetan Bhagat keeps the secret out till the last page and I loved it.

Out of three, The Girl in Room 105 was my favourite. The way the story unfolds, page by page revealing its true plot to us. Taking us from romanticism to terrorism, excellent depiction of the on ground diversity in India. 

Chetan Bhagat can undoubtedly be regarded as the writer who brought back reading culture in India. 

My favourite of his are, 

Keshav Rajpurohit series

Revolution 2020

One night at the call centre (except for the climax)

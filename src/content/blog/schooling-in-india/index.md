# Schooling in India

Government of India has recently launched a new National Education Policy (NEP) after 36 years of the launch of last policy. Although it is yet to be enacted as law, states are responding to policy and ramping up new ventures to meet the aspirations. In this regard I would like put forth some ideas I have come across from various sources for betterment of schooling.

## Profit motive in Education

Providing education in India is non-profitable and people never questioned it since it sounds correct on moral grounds but practically it is not yielding results. One must remember that a profit can only be earned when the service provided by the enterprise adds value to the customer's life. For example Google, Facebook, Starbucks, KFC add value to our lives and in return we are ready to give them profits. How difficult would a life be without Google! Public get a chance to choose and this forces them to constantly improve. Speaking of schooling, it requires 36 different licences for someone to start a school and attaining them is no way easier than summiting the Mt Everest! As a result most of the educational institutions end up being owned by politicians or someone from 'inside' the government. For someone with great ideals and determination to improve a child's life there is neither an incentive nor a common ground. 

## Desirable changes in RTE

One of the toughest norm in the RTE act for the teachers is to finish the syllabus. It is the single most important issue on teacher's agenda, more important than realsing whether the child is following or not. I wish this norm be changed to something which pays more attention to the learning outcomes of the child. It is hard for all the students to catch up at the same pace, especially in mathematics. I would strongly advice that curriculum be designed with more emphasis on teaching atleast until the child reaches 7th class. 

## Alternate approach to teaching in Government schools

Government teachers earn a lot compared to their counter parts in private schools, yet the results which private produce are unparalleled to government schools. A new approach to the teaching at government schools would be to employ 10th pass and 12th pass students for the children of classes 1st to 3rd. The basics can even be taught without a degree and an initial training period of 3 months would suffice. Later, after 2 years they can be assessed based on the child's performance and shortlisted for the B.Ed. This way we can create employment opportunities for a large chunk of 10th passouts and also give a boost to the rural women who can't afford Intermediate or Diploma. This will reduce the number of namesake teachers who only join for the sake of salary.

## Transportation service

Many a times when I travel to some village I see school children walking barefoot under scorching sun to their school which is some 3-4 kms away. The government must start a school bus service for children from same village. Better than having a school in each village with inefficient and irresponsible teaching staff is having a bus carry children to a distant but better run school. This seriously reduce operation and construction costs of government schools and also help a great deal to children.

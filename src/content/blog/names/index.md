# Names. 

What’s your name? First Name? Last Name? Middle Name? Surname? Maiden Name? Nickname? Preferred Name? Dream name? 
phew so many names!

Are they even necessary? What is the fundamental purpose of a name? It is to call or address someone specifically without ambiguity or confusion. 
But if you’re collecting so many parts of my names under different sub-headings, I’m afraid I won’t even recognize when you call out to me later!

My full name is DODDI VENKATA MANOJ KUMAR. It carries legacy, family brand and a look into my parents taste during those days — all packed into one. I’d prefer someone calling me MANOJ. Simple. But several online forms don’t get this. They collect my name under a hundred different sub-headings and end up addressing me Mr.DODDI. It’s the least preferred version of my name. 

I’ve observed that sign-up forms on internet always collect names in two parts — First Name, Last Name and usually call you with your Last Name, as is custom of respect in western world. 

But it’s not the same in the rest of the world. It’s a shame that although internet is thought to be the most democratic space in the world, it doesn’t care for inclusivity.  
If I were to be a product manager in future, I’ll have the sign-up form on my product with only two fields — Full Name and Preferred Name. Enough. 

The day the products start using this version, it shall be my day of liberation.

Weird fact: Telugu naming conventions follow ‘Surname+Given Name’ sequence and it is similar to the versions followed by Japanese, Vietnamese and Korean people. Geographically, they’re ridiculously far away compared to the Hindi belt or Malayali lands. I wonder what brings two foreign lands together in their naming conventions — definitely there would be a story hidden somewhere on internet.
